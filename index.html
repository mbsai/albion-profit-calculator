Global Settings

Premium accounts pay 4% sales tax; non-premium pay 8%. Setup fee (2.5%) is charged when you place a sell order, win or not. These feed every tab below.

Refining Setup

Default ratios follow the standard recipe (T2: 1 raw, no prev-tier bar; T3: 2 raw + 1 T2 bar; T4: 3 raw + 1 T3 bar; ... T8: 7 raw + 1 T7 bar) — edit if your setup differs.
Bonus stacking (source: wiki.albiononline.com): bonuses add up, then convert to return rate as bonus / (1 + bonus). Every royal city (Caerleon, Bridgewatch, Lymhurst, Martlock, Fort Sterling, Thetford) gets the +18% base production bonus; Brecilien gets none (not part of the royal continent). The +40% specialization bonus only applies in the one city that refines that material: Ore → Thetford, Wood → Fort Sterling, Fiber → Lymhurst, Hide → Martlock. Caerleon gets the base bonus only — no refining specialty. Focus (+59%) stacks on top wherever you use it. Defaults: no bonus = 15.3% return, specialized city = 36.7%, +Focus = 43.5% / 53.9%.

Recommended Pipeline

Step 1 — Where to Buy Materials (cheapest first)

Sourcing both the raw material and the previous-tier bar from whichever city is cheapest right now. Volume = avg units traded/day, last 7 days — low volume means thin order books even if the price looks great.

Step 2 — Where to Refine (return-rate bonus by city)

Material cost uses the cheapest-sourced raw/prev prices from Step 1. The only thing that changes per city here is your effective return rate, since the specialization bonus only applies in the matching city (Caerleon gets its own separate bonus).

Step 3 — Where to Sell (ranked by profit velocity)

Ranked by Profit/Day = profit per unit × avg daily volume traded (last 7 days) — this is what answers "which city pays back faster," not raw price. A city with the highest sticker price but thin volume (e.g. Caerleon) can lag well behind a city with a smaller margin but much heavier trading (e.g. Lymhurst, Thetford). "Est. days to sell batch" = batch size ÷ daily volume.
Note on listing age: the AODP API only exposes aggregate min/max prices and trade volume — it does not expose individual order IDs or the in-game "days remaining" countdown on a specific listing (that 30-day expiry timer is only visible by inspecting an order directly in-game). "Last Updated" below is the last time the cheapest/highest price changed, which is a proxy for data freshness, not how long that exact order has sat unsold.

Scan Setup

Materials: Ore/Wood/Fiber/Hide + refined goods, T2–T8, enchant .0–.4 (216 items). Weapons: all one- and two-handed weapon types + off-hands, T4–T8, enchant .0–.4. Armor: Head/Chest/Shoes × Cloth/Leather/Plate × all 3 set skins, T4–T8, enchant .0–.4. Accessories: bags & capes, T4–T8, enchant .0–.4. Consumables: potions & food, T4–T8, enchant .0–.4 (each potion/food only exists at some of those tiers — e.g. Healing Potion is T4/T6 only — non-existent tiers are skipped automatically). All categories use quality 1 (Normal) only — including all 5 qualities would multiply the request count ~5x. Not scanned: mounts (don't follow this tier/enchant pattern), faction-reskinned gear (Avalon/Undead/Hellgate variants), and artifact/unique/quest items. With everything enabled this is ~2,400 items and ~30 batched price requests — expect the scan to take up to a minute. Min Volume filters out routes where either city trades fewer than that many units/day on average (last 7 days) — raise it to cut illiquid noise.

Market Flip Setup

Buy side always assumes you instant-buy at the source city's lowest sell order. Volume = avg units traded/day over the last 7 days at that city, used as a liquidity signal — high spread with near-zero volume is a trap.

Best City Pairs

Scan Setup

Scans the same item universe as the Market Flip tab (materials, weapons, armor, accessories, consumables — T4–T8 or T2–T8 for materials, enchant .0–.4, quality 1) and checks each one against the Black Market's standing buy order. In practice: raw/refined materials never have a Black Market order (Black Market doesn't buy resources), while almost every weapon/armor/bag/cape does — Black Market is the game's main loot sink, so it'll quote a buy price on nearly any gear piece even if barely anyone is actually selling there right now. That's why the volume columns matter more here than the profit number: in testing, the single highest "profit" hit traded only ~1 unit/day on Black Market (a stale snapshot from one old listing), while a much more modest-looking result a few rows down was moving 400+ units/day. Raise Min Volume if the top rows look implausibly profitable. Black Market has no sell orders, only buy orders: you instant-sell into buy_price_max, there's nothing to place and wait on.
